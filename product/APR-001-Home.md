Version: 0.1



Status: Draft



Owner: Project Atlas



Created: 2026-07-10



\---



\# 1. Purpose



The Atlas Home Dashboard is the first screen every investor sees.



Its purpose is not to display more information.



Its purpose is to reduce decision anxiety.



Atlas answers one question every morning:



"What truly matters today?"



\---



\# 2. Product Vision



Atlas is not another stock application.



Atlas is a capital market knowledge operating system.



The Home Dashboard provides structured, verified and personalized market knowledge instead of fragmented news.



\---



\# 3. Design Principles



\## Principle 1



Reduce Noise



Users should never feel overwhelmed.



\---



\## Principle 2



Reduce Decision Cost



Every module should help users understand what deserves attention.



\---



\## Principle 3



Evidence First



Every conclusion should be traceable to evidence.



\---



\## Principle 4



No Prediction



Atlas never predicts prices.



Atlas explains relationships.



\---



\## Principle 5



Personal Relevance



The most important information is always related to the user's holdings.



\---



\# 4. Information Architecture



The Home Dashboard contains five modules.



\---



\## Module 1



Today's Most Important Events



Purpose



Display the five most influential market events.



Each event includes



\- Title



\- Importance Score



\- Related Entities



\- Evidence



\---



\## Module 2



My Holdings



Purpose



Show events related to the user's portfolio.



Each stock displays



\- Related Events



\- Influence Level



\- Timeline



\- Historical Similar Events



\---



\## Module 3



Upcoming Market Calendar



Purpose



Display important upcoming events.



Examples



\- Earnings



\- CPI



\- Interest Rate Decisions



\- Lock-up Expiration



\- Major Conferences



\---



\## Module 4



Market Context



Purpose



Help investors understand today's market environment.



Instead of showing opinions,



Atlas shows historical context.



Examples



\- Similar historical events



\- Policy evolution



\- Industry chain impact



\---



\## Module 5



Today in History



Purpose



Display important historical events that happened on the same date.



Help investors build long-term market cognition.



\---



\# 5. Future AI Features



Atlas AI should answer:



Why does this matter?



What does history tell us?



Which companies are connected?



What evidence supports this?



AI is an explanation engine.



Not a prediction engine.



\---



\# 6. Success Metrics



Users understand today's market within five minutes.



Users feel less anxious.



Users trust Atlas more than fragmented news.
