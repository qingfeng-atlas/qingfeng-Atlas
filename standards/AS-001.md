\# AS-001



\# Atlas Event Lifecycle Specification



> Every event deserves to be recorded.

>

> Every outcome deserves to be verified.



\---



\# 1. Purpose



Atlas does not record news.



Atlas records capital market events.



The purpose of AS-001 is to establish a unified standard for describing, tracking, validating and preserving every event occurring in China's capital market.



This standard serves as the foundation of every Atlas module.



\---



\# 2. Scope



AS-001 applies to all events stored inside Atlas.



Including but not limited to:



\- Listed Company Events

\- Policy Events

\- Macro Economic Events

\- Industry Events

\- Regulatory Events

\- International Events



\---



\# 3. Atlas Philosophy



Atlas believes:



Markets are not composed of stocks.



Markets are composed of events.



News disappears.



Events remain.



History creates knowledge.



Knowledge creates cognition.



\---



\# 4. Definition



An Event is defined as:



A verifiable occurrence that may influence one or more market participants over time.



Every Event must satisfy all following conditions:



✓ Has an occurrence time



✓ Has at least one trusted source



✓ Has affected object(s)



✓ Has lifecycle



✓ Can be continuously tracked



✓ Can produce an outcome



If any condition is missing,



it SHALL NOT be stored as an Atlas Event.



\---



\# 5. Event Lifecycle



Every Atlas Event follows exactly one lifecycle.



Signal



↓



Confirmation



↓



Classification



↓



Relationship



↓



Interpretation



↓



Tracking



↓



Outcome



↓



Archive



No Event may skip lifecycle stages.

\# 7. Source Rule



Atlas follows Official Source First.



Priority:



★★★★★



Government



Exchange



Regulator



Official Filing



★★★★☆



Company Announcement



★★★☆☆



Mainstream Media



★★☆☆☆



Professional Research



★☆☆☆☆



Social Media



Anonymous information SHALL NOT enter Atlas.



\---



\# 8. Evidence Rule



Every Atlas conclusion MUST reference evidence.



Atlas never generates unsupported statements.



Evidence may include:



Official Filing



Government Document



Historical Statistics



Financial Report



Public Data



Every Evidence receives a Trust Score.



\---



\# 9. Interpretation Rule



Atlas interprets.



Atlas does NOT predict.



Allowed:



What happened?



Why does it matter?



What happened historically?



What should continue to be observed?



Forbidden:



Buy



Sell



Target Price



Guaranteed Trend



\---



\# 10. Unknown Principle



Unknown is better than wrong.



Whenever sufficient evidence does not exist,



Atlas SHALL answer:



"Current evidence is insufficient."



Atlas never guesses.



\---



\# 11. Outcome Rule



Every Event must eventually produce an Outcome.



Possible Outcome:



Confirmed Positive



Confirmed Negative



Neutral



Cancelled



Not Materialized



Still Ongoing



Outcome may require months or years.



Atlas SHALL continue tracking until Outcome exists.



\---



\# 12. Archive Rule



Finished Events enter Atlas Archive.



Archived Events remain searchable forever.



Historical knowledge is permanent.



\---



\# 13. Core Values



Facts over Opinions.



Evidence over Emotion.



History over Noise.



Knowledge over Information.



Trust over Traffic.
